## About

Dr. Persephone Blue (PB) Berge is an Assistant Professor of Experimental Game Design in the Media & Technology Studies Program at the University of Alberta. She is an award-winning game designer studying the so-called "unplayable." Their work can be found in *Game Studies,* *New Media & Society,* *Feminist Media Studies* and the proceedings of DiGRA, ICIDS, and elsewhere.

*In publications, Dr. Berge's name has been stylized both as "PS Berge" and "PB Berge."*

<div id="printPageButton">

<h3>Research Areas:</h3>

<p>Games and media, trans play, critical platform research, tabletop roleplaying games, personal games, XR, critical making.</p></div>

**University of Alberta (2024-2025)**

- *A graduate-level interdisciplinary course combining media theory and hands-on technical workshops to guide students through the design and development of original digital humanist media works. **Trans & Queer Game Design** [Cross-listed course between Media & Tech Studies, Women's and Gender Studies, and Computer Science]

- *Advanced special topics course on the theory and practice of trans and queer interactive media art. Spanning videogames, interactive exhibitions, and physical computing, this course prompted students to design personal, theoretically motivated game projects and subsequently showcase these projects at an exhibition alongside an alternative controller or haptic experience that they designed.* 

**Understanding Videogames**

- *Intermediate course on the dynamics of gaming culture and game development. Students developed up to six small game experiments, wrote extensive reflections on their relationships to games and game culture, and were introduced to key critical and theoretical interventions into contemporary game studies.*
